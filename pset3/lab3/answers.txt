sort1 uses: Bubble sort

How do you know?: The time it cost to sort the sorted ones is faster than sort3, and its time to sort the reversed ones is more than sort2.

sort2 uses: Merge sort

How do you know?: Its average time to sort reversed ones is far faster than the other two sort, and the time it cost for sorted & reversed is close(n*logn).

sort3 uses: Selection sort

How do you know?: The time for sorted & reversed is close, but is way slower than sort2.
